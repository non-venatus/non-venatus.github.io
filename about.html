The Book

Non-Venatus: On the Production of Wars and Universities

Non-Venatus names and confronts a regime it calls venatus — a Latin term for hunting — which describes the institutional order through which knowledge is turned into a tool of conquest. Venatus is the structural condition that makes universities complicit in war, fascism, and the eradication of dissent.

The book proposes Non-venatus not merely as a critique but as an escape plan. It is a counter-abstraction: a way of thinking that creates space for creativity to resist capture, and for thought to break free from the violence of method.

The book draws on a rich range of thinkers including Abdullah Öcalan, the Dalai Lama, Intizar Husain, Augusto Boal, Gilles Deleuze, Félix Guattari, Gilbert Simondon, Walter D. Mignolo, Brian Massumi, John Protevi, Gerald Raunig, Mehrdad Vahabi, Arjun Appadurai, Jean Paul Sartre, Jonathan O. Chimakonam, Moishe Postone, and Alfred Sohn-Rethel.

About Read-Act

A Community of Reading and Acting

Read-Act is an independent reading and discussion community founded by Hidam Premananda and his colleagues. Running since 2018, it brings together scholars, students, artists, writers, and activists to engage collectively with philosophy, literature, and critical theory — outside the hierarchies and constraints of conventional academic institutions.

The community runs periodic reading sessions across different seasons — Monsoon, Spring, Winter — each focused on a set of texts, ideas, or provocations. Sessions have ranged from readings of Deleuze and Guattari to discussions of peace, feminism, microfascism, and the philosophy of running.

What Read-Act does

Sessions — Read-Act has run over twenty reading sessions since its founding, across multiple seasons, bringing together people around shared intellectual questions. Past sessions have engaged with themes including subversive time (Aion), cellular power, feminist theory, delirium, critique, public feelings, microfascism, peace, and revolution.

Essays — Members contribute essays across philosophy, literature, and aphorism — from "Microfascism" and "Stop Decoding the Cellular Power" to "Revolution as Insensate" and literary reflections on teaching, fighting truthfully, and running.

Podcast — Read-Act produces a podcast, extending its conversations beyond the reading room to a wider audience.

The Non-Venatus Project — Non-Venatus: On the Production of Wars and Universities emerges directly from the intellectual community built through Read-Act. The book represents a crystallisation of years of collective reading, debate, and fugitive thinking.

Author

Hidam Premananda

Hidam Premananda is an independent scholar based in India. He previously taught political philosophy at the Department of Political Science, University of Delhi. His intellectual work spans critical theory, postcolonial philosophy, peace studies, and the philosophy of resistance.

He is the co-creator of the Read-Act community with his colleagues, which has served as the intellectual home for much of the thinking in this book. Non-Venatus is his first monograph with Bloomsbury Academic.
